DEFRA Requirements

Category 1 (Low Risk)

Materials posing minimal risk. May be imported without a specific Letter of Authority (LoA), but robust packaging is still mandatory.

Example: Fully processed/manufactured plant fibers.

Category 2 (Medium Risk)

Materials that may host pests/diseases (e.g., raw plant wastes). Requires LoA from Plant Health Service + Triple Packaging.

Example: Dried Olive Pomace, Straw, Banana Fibers.

Category 3 (High Risk)

Soil, compost, or untreated animal by-products. Usually prohibited without strict justification & treatment.

Example: Raw/Untreated Poultry Feathers.

Packaging & Labelling (Mandatory)

1

Primary Container

Sealed, leak-proof bag/jar containing the sample.

2

Secondary Container

Leak-proof box/bag acting as a barrier.

3

Tertiary Container

Strong outer box labeled with LoA Reference.

Important: Shipments must travel directly to the authorised laboratory. No intermediate opening or repackaging allowed.

3.3 Letter of Authority (LoA)

  • Formal authorisation from DEFRA/APHA for Cat 2 & 3.
  • Must state research purpose, quantity, and specific conditions.
  • Customs: Evidence of LoA must be provided during clearance.

3.4 Pre-Shipment Screening

Required data to demonstrate safety (the "Data Sheet"):

Moisture < 10% pH Microbio Screen Water Activity (aw)

Materials must be dehydrated (e.g., 60¬∞C to constant weight) to prevent mould.

3.5 Import Pathway

  • Direct Route: Origin → UK Authorised Facility.
  • Chain of Custody: Keep rigorous records.
  • Declaration: Label as "Research samples, non-commercial".

3.6 Poultry Feathers (Special Rules)

Untreated feathers are High Risk. Import is usually refused without an Export Health Certificate proving sterilisation.

ACE Section 3 Checklist

Section 3 Question Interpretation & Recommended Action
Material description, origin & amount

Action: Be precise. E.g., "Dried olive pomace (Olea europaea), Spain, 0.5 kg".

Keep quantities small (research-scale) to justify non-commercial status.
Is the material a controlled substance?

Answer: No (unless using hemp/poppy). Confirm materials are standard agricultural by-products.
Has the material been approved by customs?

Action: Attach the LoA from DEFRA/APHA. Cite the import reference number.
Contains fine particulate matter (<10¬µm)?

Context: Dust inhalation risk. If milled, acknowledge potential for dust.

Action: Ensure packaging is dust-tight. For fibers/straw, note "Coarse material, minimal dust".
Has the material been sterilised/treated?

Action: Specify method. E.g., "Dehydrated at 60¬∞C for 48h". For feathers: "Chemically sanitised & steam treated".
Recent pathogenic outbreaks in origin?

Action: Check DEFRA alerts. State "None known affecting this material" if accurate. Cite sources if possible.
Exposed to herbicides/pesticides?

Action: If organic, say "No". If unknown, state "Residual agricultural waste; standard food-safety limits apply".
Will product deteriorate with time?

Answer: No (if dried properly). Note: "Low moisture content (<10%) prevents decay".
Requires special storage conditions?

Action: Usually "Cool, dry place in sealed containers".
Contains common allergens?

Action: Flag if relevant (e.g., Wheat straw). Label containers with "Potential Allergen".
Cleaned of soil and debris?

Action: Confirm "Yes". Soil is High Risk (Cat 3). State materials were "sieved/washed prior to drying".

Frequently Asked Questions

What is an LoA and how do I get one?

A Letter of Authority (LoA) is a licence from DEFRA allowing the import of prohibited material for research. You must apply via the PEACH system or IPAFFS, detailing the material, quantity, and research purpose.

Can I import untreated feathers?

Generally, no. They are high-risk Animal By-Products. You must have an Export Health Certificate proving sterilisation, or the shipment will likely be destroyed.

What if my packaging is damaged?

If the triple-layer seal is broken upon arrival, the lab may refuse acceptance to prevent contamination. Ensure tertiary packaging is heavy-duty.

Who helps with this at Bath?

Consult the Department Safety Officer (DSO) or the technical manager of the specific lab (e.g., BRE Building) before arranging shipment.
