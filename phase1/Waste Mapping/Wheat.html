üîç Executive Summary

This scientific dataset aggregates national data for Wheat (Triticum spp.) production in Lebanon to model agricultural residue availability. Based on FAO (2023) conservative baselines (100,000 t grain) and the IPCC Tier-1 residue-to-product ratio (1.2), we define the national waste baseline at 120,000 tonnes of straw/year. The regional distribution is highly concentrated in the Bekaa and South Lebanon, validated by multi-source triangulation (MoA, FAO, FEWS NET).

2. Waste Generation Methodology

A Calculation Models

Straw Generation Formula

$$ P_{\text{straw}} = 100,000 \times 1.2 = 120,000 \text{ t/year} $$
üí°

IPCC Factor 1.2: Conservative global default. Regional studies (Zhang et al.) suggest a range of 1.0‚Äì1.5, confirming 1.2 as a robust central estimate.

B Data Interpretation

  • 1

    Primary Source

    FAOSTAT 2023 (Ref Year 2023) provides the 100,000t baseline. Flagged as unofficial but accepted for Tier-1 assessment.

  • 2

    Residue Definition

    Refers to dry straw suitable for collection. Roots and stubble are excluded from this extractable biomass figure.

3. Regional Waste Distribution

Allocation Method: Primary (Saab et al./MoA) > Validation (FEWS NET) > Residual Proxy (AUB/Land Share)

$$ P_{\text{straw},i} = 120,000 \times \frac{\text{Wheat Share}_i}{100} $$

Where wheat-specific regional production data is unavailable, residual shares are distributed proportionally using agricultural land distribution as a proxy. No artificial precision is introduced beyond this level.

5 Key Methodological Insights

  • ‚óè Primary Concentration: Wheat straw availability is highly localized. Bekaa and Baalbek-Hermel together account for 58%, while South Lebanon contributes 30%, meaning 88% of national straw potential is concentrated in these cereal-producing regions.
  • ‚óè Robust Factor: The IPCC Tier-1 ratio (1.2) is widely accepted for national inventories. Regional sensitivity analysis (1.0‚Äì1.5) confirms this is a safe, conservative baseline for industrial planning.
  • ‚óè Land Proportionality: For minor regions (Akkar, North, Nabatieh), allocations use agricultural land shares (AUB 2020) to distribute the residual 12% where direct crop statistics are absent.
  • ‚óè FEWS NET Validation: FEWS NET (2024) data reflects conflict-affected seasons and is used here to validate structural regional importance rather than recalibrate long-term production averages.
