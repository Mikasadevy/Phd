üîç Executive Summary

This scientific dataset quantifies banana biomass waste in Lebanon, focusing on the coastal cultivation of Musa spp. By synthesizing 2023 production data (82,311 tonnes) with global biomass factors (4:1 ratio for pseudostems/leaves), we establish a national waste baseline of 329,246 tonnes/year. The methodology employs a hybrid allocation model, anchoring 53% of waste in the South and 35% in Mount Lebanon (Damour), ensuring precise regional targeting for biomaterial applications.

2. Waste Generation Methodology

A Calculation Models

Total Biomass Calculation

$$ P_{\text{biomass}} = 82,311.48 \times 4.0 = 329,245.92 \approx 329,246 \text{ t/year} $$
üí°

Factor 4.0: Accounts for pseudostems (trunk), leaves, and rachis. Pseudostems alone account for ~3 tonnes per tonne of fruit.

B Biomass Components

  • 1

    Pseudostem (Trunk)

    Major fibrous waste. Approx 3 tonnes per tonne of fruit. High value for textiles/construction.

  • 2

    Leaves & Rachis

    Remainder of biomass. Often field residues.

  • i

    Peel Fraction

    ~10-20% of fruit mass. Included within the overall 4:1 system boundary for total organic waste.

5 Key Methodological Insights

  • ‚óè High Yield Waste: Banana biomass (4:1 ratio) is one of the highest-yield waste streams per hectare among Lebanese crops, making it ideal for industrial biomaterials.
  • ‚óè Regional Concentration: The South (174,500 t/y) is the primary target for partnerships, followed closely by the coastal belt of Mount Lebanon (Damour).
  • ‚óè Agro-Climatic Exclusion: Continental regions (Bekaa, Baalbek-Hermel, Akkar) are correctly modeled at 0% due to frost risk and altitude constraints.
