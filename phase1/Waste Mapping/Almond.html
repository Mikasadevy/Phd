üîç Executive Summary

This scientific dataset quantifies Almond waste in Lebanon for the reference year 2023. Based on FAOSTAT production data of 29,309 tonnes and authoritative regional areas from the World Bank, the model deducts kernel exports to establish a net domestic supply of ~29,110 tonnes. The analysis identifies two major biomass streams: Processing Waste (Hulls & Shells: ~24,742 t) and Agricultural Waste (Prunings: ~7,271 t). These streams are heavily concentrated in the inland regions of Baalbek-Hermel (50%) and Akkar (23%), offering high potential for bio-energy and compost valorization.

2. Waste Generation Methodology

A Processing Fractions

Based on Mediterranean Almond Standards

1. Hulls (Green Outer) 52%
2. Shells (Hard Inner) 33%
3. Kernel (Product) 15%

Citations:

‚Ä¢ Hull 52%, Shell 33%, Kernel 15% ‚Äî MDPI (2025), Almond Hulls & Shells Review.

‚Ä¢ Pruning biomass = 1.34 t/ha ‚Äî Renewable Energy (2011), Mediterranean Pruning Biomass Study.

B Calculation Logic

Net Domestic Almonds Formula

$$ \text{Net Domestic} = 29,309.03 - 199.27 = 29,109.76 \text{ t} $$

Processing Waste Formula

$$ W_{proc} = (P_{prod} - P_{exp\_eq}) \times 0.85 $$ $$ W_{proc} = 29,109.76 \times 0.85 \approx 24,742 \text{ t} $$

Pruning Biomass

$$ W_{pruning} = \text{Area} \times 1.34 \text{ t/ha} $$ $$ W_{pruning} = 5,427 \times 1.34 \approx 7,272 \text{ t} $$

3. Regional Waste Distribution

A Source of Regional Shares

Regional almond distribution is derived directly from Table 3.4 (Fruit Tree Cultivation Areas by Governorate) in the World Bank report: Droughts and Agriculture in Lebanon: Causes, Consequences, and Risk Management (2018). This table provides the definitive area dataset (in hectares) for almonds across Lebanon‚Äôs governorates.

B Methodology Formula

$$ \text{Regional Share (\%)} = (\frac{\text{Regional Almond Area}}{5,427 \text{ ha total}}) \times 100 $$

C Logic of Waste Location

Because almond shelling and hulling typically occur on-farm or in local village processing units, waste (hulls, shells, pruning residues) is assumed to be generated in the same governorate where the almonds are cultivated. No evidence exists for large-scale inter-governorate processing flows.

D Waste Computation Formula

$$ \text{Regional Production} = \text{Net Domestic Production} \times \text{Regional Share} $$

$$ \text{Hulls} = \text{Regional Production} \times 0.52 $$

$$ \text{Shells} = \text{Regional Production} \times 0.33 $$

$$ \text{Pruning} = \text{Regional Area} \times 1.34 \text{ t/ha} $$
