üîç Executive Summary

This dashboard presents a rigorous assessment of Chicken Feather Waste in Lebanon. Anchored by the official FAOSTAT 2023 broiler meat production baseline (121,706.90 t), we derive a national feather waste estimate of ~7,302 tonnes/year. Due to the lack of disaggregated regional data, the spatial analysis employs a qualitative intensity methodology, identifying the Bekaa and North Lebanon as critical clusters based on slaughterhouse and farm density.

2. Waste Calculation Methodology

A The 6% Factor

Scientific consensus places feather weight at 5‚Äì7% of total broiler live weight. We adopt the midpoint of 6% for LCA and rendering design purposes.

Methodological Honesty Statement

Lebanon lacks published numerical data for feather tonnage by governorate. To avoid false precision, we present a qualitative intensity model based on the location of major slaughterhouses (e.g., Tanmia, Chekka) and farm density reports (MoA, IDAL).

Region Feather Waste Intensity Key Evidence & Drivers
Bekaa VERY HIGH Tanmia slaughterhouse; intensive broiler farms; high manure generation zones.
North Lebanon HIGH Chekka industrial slaughterhouse expansion; historic broiler capacity hub.
Mount Lebanon HIGH Hosts ~45% of agro-industrial enterprises; significant processing activity.
Akkar MEDIUM Documented poultry farms (e.g., Makary Chicken); rural production.
South Lebanon MEDIUM Significant broiler activity; FAO (2025) reports large conflict-related capacity.
Nabatieh MEDIUM Cluster with South; moderate farm density.
Beirut LOW / NEGLIGIBLE Minimal primary poultry production.
