Paper and cardboard waste in Lebanon originates from a combination of commercial packaging, institutional use, and household MSW streams. Unlike agricultural wastes, national quantities are partially reported through municipal solid waste composition studies and recycling sector reports, while regional tonnages remain weakly disaggregated.

2. Evidence Anchors: Municipal Waste Audits

Sector Reporting & Studies

  • 1
    SWEEP-Net Reports (2014, 2016): Identify paper and cardboard as approximately 16-17% of the total municipal solid waste stream in Lebanon.
  • 2
    World Bank & MoE Studies: Corroborate the composition data, highlighting higher concentrations of paper waste in urban centers due to commercial activity and higher consumption rates.
  • 3
    Recycling Sector Activity: Established paper mills and recovery facilities (e.g., Solicar, Sicomo) provide operational evidence of significant recoverable volumes, though facility-level throughput data is proprietary.

4. Methodology

Paper & cardboard waste estimates are derived from national MSW composition studies and recycling sector reports. Unlike agricultural residues, waste generation is driven by consumption patterns rather than production geography; therefore, only national quantities are reported, while regional mapping remains qualitative.
