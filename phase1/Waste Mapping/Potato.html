üîç Executive Summary

This scientific dataset quantifies Potato (Item 01510) waste in Lebanon using a net-trade methodology. By isolating national production and deducting export volumes, the model calculates the specific organic load remaining within the domestic system. The analysis confirms that commercial potato production and its associated agricultural waste are structurally concentrated in two key regions: Bekaa (70%) and Akkar (30%).

Export Context & Markets

Exports represent high-quality, sorted produce that leaves the national boundaries. To avoid overestimating domestic waste generation, these volumes are removed from the baseline. Major markets include the UAE, Kuwait, Oman, Qatar, and Iraq.

2. Agricultural Waste Methodology

Calculation Model

$$ \text{Supply}_{\text{net}} = 659,588 - 59,988 = 599,600 \text{ t} $$ $$ \text{Ag\_Waste} = \text{Supply}_{\text{net}} \times 0.525 \approx 314,790 \text{ t/year} $$

The aggregate loss factor of 0.525 represents the sum of haulm residue (0.35), culls (0.075), and post-harvest losses (0.10) applied to the domestic supply.
