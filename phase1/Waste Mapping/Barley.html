üîç Executive Summary

This scientific dataset quantifies Barley (Hordeum vulgare) residues in Lebanon, focusing on the semi-arid, high-altitude production zones. Based on FAO (2023) production data (30,000 t grain) and a conservative Residue-to-Product Ratio (RPR) of 0.90, we define the national waste baseline at 27,000 tonnes of straw/year. The regional distribution is highly concentrated based on cultivated area distribution, with Baalbek-Hermel accounting for ~80.4% of the national cultivated barley area.

2. Waste Generation Methodology

A Calculation Models

Straw Generation Formula

$$ P_{\text{straw}} = 30,000 \times 0.90 = 27,000 \text{ t/year} $$
üí°

Factor 0.90: Lower than wheat (1.2) due to barley's physiology and dryland cultivation context. Compatible with IPCC guidelines for semi-arid systems.

B Data Interpretation

  • 1

    Agro-Climatic Context

    Barley is concentrated in high-altitude, low-rainfall zones (Northern Bekaa). It is drought-tolerant.

  • 2

    Residue Use

    Almost all barley straw enters the animal feed chain (tibn). Only ~1% is burned (UNFCCC BUR2).

5 Key Methodological Insights

  • ‚óè High Concentration: Barley is Lebanon's most localized cereal crop. Baalbek-Hermel alone dominates with 80.4% of cultivated area due to its semi-arid climate suitability.
  • ‚óè Conservative RPR: The 0.90 factor is lower than wheat, reflecting the shorter stature and stress-tolerant physiology of barley in dryland conditions.
  • ‚óè Area-Based Allocation: Regional shares are derived strictly from cultivated area statistics (World Bank 2010), serving as a Tier-1 proxy for residue generation. FAO‚ÄìWFP (2024) validates the overall dominance pattern.
