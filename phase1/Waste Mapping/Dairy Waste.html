üîç Executive Summary

Dairy whey is one of Lebanon‚Äôs largest liquid organic waste streams. Anchored by a national milk production baseline of 270,000 tonnes/year (FAO 2011), this dashboard quantifies whey generation at 220,000 tonnes/year (UNOPS 2025). This high-BOD effluent is heavily concentrated in the Bekaa and Baalbek-Hermel regions (60%), reflecting the location of major industrial processors and livestock intensity, with significant secondary clusters in the South and Mount Lebanon.

2. Calculation Methodology

1 Processing Volume

$$ M_{\text{processed}} = 0.60 \times 270,000 = 162,000 \text{ t} $$

Only 60% of raw milk enters the processing chain (FAO 2011).

2 Whey Output

$$ M_{\text{whey}} \approx 220,000 \text{ t} $$ $$ \text{Ratio} \approx 1.35 \times M_{\text{processed}} $$

Note: The >100% ratio (1.35) reflects the high volume of liquid whey generated relative to solid product, aligned with UNOPS (2025) estimates.

3. Regional Distribution

‚úÖ Hierarchical Regional Allocation

Regional dairy waste shares are estimated using livestock concentration, producer density, processing capacity, and conflict-loss proxies due to the absence of direct regional milk production data; therefore, allocations are reported with medium confidence.

  • Herd Concentration: Bekaa holds ~72% sheep / 51% goats (Mercy Corps).
  • Processing Hubs: Major processors in Bekaa/Baalbek.
  • Conflict Impact: Distress sales/loss in South/Nabatieh (UNDP 2024).

üìå Allocation Defense

Bekaa (40%)

Primary hub. 72% of national sheep/51% goats. Major industrial processors (LibanLait, Khoury).

Baalbek-Hermel (20%)

Livestock intensive extension of Bekaa. Highest number of small-ruminant producers.

South (15%)

Secondary zone (~150k goats). Significant distress sales and informal processing waste.

Nabatieh (10%)

Pastoral activity similar to South. Conflict-related livestock loss increases waste.

Mount Lebanon (10%)

Processing-driven. High volume of cheese/labneh production relative to farm size.

North (5%)

Low-density pastoral contribution. Smaller scale than Bekaa/South.
