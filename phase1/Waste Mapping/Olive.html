This scientific dataset aggregates authoritative data on Lebanon‚Äôs olive sector, establishing a rigorous baseline for pomace waste generation. By synthesizing data from the Ministry of Agriculture (MoA), FAO, and peer-reviewed studies (Kinab & Khoury), we define the national waste baseline at 79,000 tonnes/year. The methodology accounts for extraction technologies dominated by traditional and 3-phase mills, resulting in a high effective waste fraction, and distributes this load regionally.

A Calculation Models

Theoretical Mass Balance

Lebanon-Specific Empirical Fraction

$$ f_{pomace,effective} = \frac{79,000}{136,176} \approx 0.58 \text{ (58\%)} $$

5 Key Methodological Insights

  • ‚óè Technology Factor: Lebanon‚Äôs high pomace fraction is consistent with the dominance of traditional presses and 3-phase mills, which retain more water in the solid waste than modern 2-phase systems.
  • ‚óè Distribution Proxy: Regional waste distribution relies on MoA‚Äôs official olive cultivation area. This is the strongest available indicator of milling density as mill-level production statistics are unpublished.
  • ‚óè FAO Compliance: FAO biomass assessment guidelines specifically endorse surface area as a reliable proxy for waste modeling when sub-national production statistics are unavailable.
