The MCDA Methodology

A structured six-step process ensuring transparency, replicability, and academic rigor

1
üìã

Define Criteria & Weights

Established 7 weighted criteria reflecting research priorities: Technical (30%), Environmental (15%), Market (15%), Policy (10%), Logistics (5%), Collaboration (15%), Funder Alignment (10%)

2
üìä

Define Indicators

Translated criteria into measurable indicators (e.g., Biomass Potential, Waste Mismanagement %, LPI Scores). Each indicator weighted within its criterion

3
üîç

Collect Data

Gathered from authoritative sources: FAO, World Bank (WDI, WGI, LPI), DEFRA, National Inventory Reports, peer-reviewed literature, government dashboards

4
‚öñÔ∏è

Normalize & Score (1-5)

Quantitative: ranking-based (highest=5, lowest=1). Qualitative: categorical scoring (Very High=5, High=4, Moderate=3, Low=2, Very Low=1)

5
‚ö°

Apply Weights & Aggregate

Multiplied each indicator score by its weight, aggregated to criterion scores, then applied criterion weights for final weighted sum

6
üèÜ

Rank & Interpret

Final ranking reveals Lebanon (#1, 391.66), Brazil (#2, 348.81), Indonesia (#3, 325.61), UK (#4, 320.85), KSA (#5, 315.16). Tested sensitivity
