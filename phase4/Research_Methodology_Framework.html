PhD Phase 4

Literature Intelligence
Methodology Framework

Converting fragmented literature into an auditable decision-support system.

Objective

Traditional narrative reviews often succumb to subjective summarization. Phase 4 replaces this with a structured data extraction pipeline.

The goal is to convert fragmented scientific literature into an auditable decision-support system, ensuring that every experimental parameter in the PhD is derived from rigorous evidence rather than arbitrary selection.

End-to-End Workflow

1

Broad Discovery

Aggregating records from multiple databases (Scopus, Springer, WoS) using standardized keyword strings to capture the full landscape of the waste stream.

2

Screening & Filtering

Applying strict inclusion/exclusion criteria to remove irrelevant domains (e.g., nutritional science) and focus purely on material characterization and construction applications.

3

Structured Data Extraction

Decomposing PDF papers into raw JSON data files. Capturing quantitative mix designs, processing parameters, and mechanical results without narrative interpretation.

4

Synthesis & Analysis

Aggregating JSON files to identify cross-paper trends, optimal replacement ranges, and methodological contradictions across the dataset.

5

Experimental Strategy

Translating synthesized insights into specific lab protocols, defining control variables and target performance metrics for the PhD experiments.

Structured Extraction Philosophy

The Old Way: Summarization

Traditional reviews rely on reading abstracts and writing summaries. This introduces bias, loses numeric precision, and makes cross-comparison difficult as data remains locked in text.

Phase 4: Decomposition

Papers are treated as data sources. We extract specific entities (e.g., "Calcination Temp: 600Â°C", "Compressive Strength: 45MPa") into machine-readable JSON formats. This allows us to query the literature like a database.

Academic Integrity & Tools

Role of LLMs (Assistive Only)

Large Language Models are utilized strictly as extraction and formatting assistants. They parse unstructured text to populate the JSON schema.

Crucial Bound: LLMs do not determine relevance, write the analysis, or decide experimental parameters. All validation, interpretation, and strategic decisions remain 100% researcher-controlled.

Analysis & Interpretation

Once data is structured, the analysis layer shifts from reading to querying. We look for:

Consensus

Where do multiple independent studies agree on a material property or optimal processing range?

Contradiction

Where do results diverge, suggesting hidden variables (e.g., origin location) that need controlling?

Gaps

Which combinations of treatments and matrices have never been tested, offering novelty?

From Literature to Experimental Strategy

The final output of Phase 4 is not a "Review Paper" but an Experimental Protocol. Every variable chosen for the upcoming lab work from the calcination temperature to the replacement percentage is directly traced back to the evidence synthesized in this phase.
