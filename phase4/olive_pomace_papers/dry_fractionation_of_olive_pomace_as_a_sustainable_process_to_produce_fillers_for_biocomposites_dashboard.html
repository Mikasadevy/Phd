‚öôÔ∏è Processing Routes Investigated

Route B: Dry Friction (Best Performer)

Recommended
Equipment Ball Mill (MM 400)
Frequency 15 Hz
Duration 2 minutes

Mechanism: Friction solicitations detach pulp from stone without crushing the stone, followed by sieving (1mm mesh). High yield (99.4%) with no water use.

Wet Fractionation (Control)

Water washing + Sieving

  • High Purity
  • Low Yield (82.1%)
  • Consumes Water (5:1 ratio)

Route A: Grinding & Sieving

Knife Mill + Sieving

  • Lower Purity
  • Complex Setup
  • Stone fragmentation issues

Yield Comparison

99.4% Dry Friction
vs 82.1% (Wet Fractionation)

Dry process avoids material loss to effluents.

Purity Trade-off

Dry friction yields stone fractions (dS3) with slightly higher pulp residue (higher ash/lignin) compared to wet washing, but significantly better environmental profile.
