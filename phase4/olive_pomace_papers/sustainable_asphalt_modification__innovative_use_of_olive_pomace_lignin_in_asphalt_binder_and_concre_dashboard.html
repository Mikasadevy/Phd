üéì Core Takeaway ‚úì Extracted

10% Olive Pomace Lignin replacement of asphalt binder increases Marshall stability by 29% and softening point to 57¬∞C, though higher dosages compromise ductility. The authors extracted lignin from olive pomace using acid hydrolysis and incorporated it into AC 60/70 asphalt binder at dosages of 0, 4, 6, 8, 10, 12, and 14% by weight. Mechanical and rheological tests (Penetration, Softening Point, Viscosity, Marshall Stability) showed that dosages >10% caused reduced ductility and phase separation issues.
