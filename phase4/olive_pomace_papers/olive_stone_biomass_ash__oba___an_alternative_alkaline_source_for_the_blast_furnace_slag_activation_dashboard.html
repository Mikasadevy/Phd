Summary of Methodology

‚úì Extracted

The authors studied the use of olive-stone biomass ash (OBA) as an alkaline activator for blast furnace slag (BFS) in mortars. They prepared two series of OBA mixtures: one where OBA replaced BFS (15-35%) and one where OBA was added to BFS (5-25%). These were compared against control mortars activated with commercial KOH and NaOH solutions (4-14 mol/kg). Specimens were cured at 65¬∞C for 7 days.

Key Result: 25% OBA addition yielded the highest compressive strength (38.38 MPa), exceeding the best performing KOH (25.56 MPa) and NaOH (31.11 MPa) controls.

Mechanisms

  • Pore Refinement: OBA systems showed lower mean pore diameter (11.9 nm for OBA-Ad25) compared to KOH systems.
  • Mineralogy: Scarcer formation of zeolite structures in OBA systems (no katoite or K-I zeolite observed).
