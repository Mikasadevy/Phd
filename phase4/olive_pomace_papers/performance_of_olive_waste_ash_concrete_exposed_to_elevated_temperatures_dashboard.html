üéì Executive Summary ‚úì Extracted

The authors incinerated olive mill waste at 800¬∞C to produce Olive Waste Ash (OWA). They replaced cement with 7%, 15%, and 22% OWA in concrete mixes with w/c ratios of 0.5 and 0.7, using basalt or tuff aggregates. Specimens were cured for 90 days and then exposed to 400¬∞C and 600¬∞C. Results showed that increasing OWA content increased residual compressive strength (RCS) after firing. Specifically, RCS at 400¬∞C increased from 52% (control) to 71% (22% OWA). Electrical charge (RCPT) decreased with increasing OWA content, indicating reduced permeability/damage.

üéØ Core Takeaway
Replacing up to 22% of cement with olive waste ash improves the residual compressive strength and reduces chloride permeability of concrete exposed to temperatures up to 600¬∞C.
