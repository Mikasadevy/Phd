‚ö†Ô∏è Limitations & Trade-offs

üî•

Low Firing Temperature

Bricks were fired at only 750 ¬∞C. This is lower than typical industrial standards, potentially resulting in lower strength compared to literature fired at higher temperatures.

üíß

Water Absorption

At 20% OPBA, water absorption reaches 28%, which exceeds recommended levels (typically <17%) for certain applications.

üìâ

Strength Drop-off

Adding >15% OPBA results in bricks with weak compressive strength (<7 MPa) that may not satisfy standards for load-bearing walls.

Closed Loop Potential

OPBA is a byproduct of the brick industry itself (combustion of olive pomace for kiln energy). Recycling it back into the bricks creates a circular "closed loop" system, eliminating disposal costs and reducing virgin clay consumption.

Feasibility: Tested in industrial scale furnace (EXTRABRICK manufactory), indicating high readiness for industrial adoption.
