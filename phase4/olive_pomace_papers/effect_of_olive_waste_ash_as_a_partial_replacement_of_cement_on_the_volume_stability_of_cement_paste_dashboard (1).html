The study demonstrates that replacing up to 20% of cement with Olive Waste Ash (OWA) creates a viable paste with reduced shrinkage. Specifically, 10% OWA is identified as the optimal threshold, minimizing both drying and autogenous shrinkage while maintaining mechanical strength within 8% of the control.

üìä Quantitative Results

Measured Data

Optimal Threshold: 10%

10% OWA replacement minimizes shrinkage without severe strength loss. Drying shrinkage drops from 2015 ¬µm/m (Control) to 1730 ¬µm/m.

Strength Trade-off

All OWA mixes showed lower strength than control. At 10% replacement, 90-day compressive strength was 45.4 MPa vs 50 MPa for control (-9.2%).

‚ö†Ô∏è Limitations & Risks

High Replacement Risk

Replacements >15% cause severe strength loss (down to ~20 MPa) and increased drying shrinkage due to porosity.

Scope Limitation

Study limited to cement paste. Effects on concrete (with aggregates) may differ due to interfacial transition zones.

üéì PhD Relevance

Actionable Takeaway

This paper provides a high-quality dataset for modeling the non-monotonic effect of Olive Waste Ash on drying shrinkage. It establishes 10% as a critical inflection point for optimization studies.

Recommended Actions

  • Use P10% data as a benchmark for "optimal" OWA replacement.
  • Model the shrinkage dip between 5-10% and rise at >15%.
  • Replicate mixing protocol: 8h calcination + 2h milling.
