Compressive Strength vs Temperature

Strength decreases with temperature but remains >17 MPa at 750¬∞C.

Thermal Conductivity vs Temperature

Lower firing temperature yields better insulation (lower conductivity).

Energy & Insulation Trade-off

Reducing temperature to 750¬∞C lowers thermal conductivity by ~20% (0.65 to 0.52 W/mK), significantly improving insulation properties.

Porosity Impact

Lower firing temperatures led to higher water absorption (11.8% to 15.7%), indicating increased porosity due to incomplete vitrification.

üå± Sustainability & Innovation

Waste Valorisation

Direct utilization of Olive Pomace Fly Ash, a byproduct of the olive oil industry in Tunisia, preventing landfilling and dust pollution.

Process Optimization

Reduction of firing temperature from 850¬∞C to 750¬∞C. This significantly lowers the embodied energy and carbon footprint of the brick manufacturing process.

Innovation Verdict

Moderate Innovation. While ash in bricks is common, the explicit focus on temperature reduction capability (using the ash as a flux) rather than just material replacement is a valuable process optimization contribution.
