Study Limitations

  • 1.

    Simulation Only: The study adds ash to the raw meal. It does not perform actual combustion of DOP fuel in a rotary kiln, which might affect flame temperature or clinker nodulization.

  • 2.

    Low Addition Rates: Because it simulates ash residue, the actual mass added is very low (<0.4%). Higher ash fuels might have different impacts.

  • 3.

    Limited Durability: Only soundness was tested. Long-term leaching, sulfate attack, or chloride resistance data is absent.

Tier C Relevance Good for Benchmarking

Actionable Takeaway

Adding small amounts of DOP ash (up to 0.38 wt%) to raw meal to simulate 100% fuel replacement does not negatively impact clinker formation or cement strength. This study provides a "safe" green light for DOP utilization regarding mechanical properties.

Value for PhD

Provides high-quality baseline data (Tier A quality) for comparing alternative fuel ashes. Excellent reference for chemical composition of Olive Pomace Ash.

Risk

Do not over-generalize to high-volume ash additions. The results are specific to the low ash content of the fuel simulated.

üå± Sustainability & Energy

Fossil Fuel Displacement

The primary goal is substituting coal. While this paper studies the material effect of the ash, the implication is a 100% reduction in coal usage for the firing process simulated.

Waste Valorization

Utilizes Dried Olive Pomace (DOP), a major agricultural by-product in Mediterranean regions (Greece, Spain), preventing waste accumulation.
