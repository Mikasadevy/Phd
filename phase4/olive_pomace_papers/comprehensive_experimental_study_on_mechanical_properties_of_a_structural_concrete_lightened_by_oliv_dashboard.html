üéì Core Research Takeaway ‚ö° Interpretive

Partial replacement of sand with 5% dry olive pomace aggregates (OPA) mixed with olive mill wastewater yields a structural concrete with 25 MPa compressive strength. While suitable for C25/30 applications, higher substitution levels (>5%) significantly degrade mechanical performance, illustrating a strict threshold for this agricultural waste stream in structural applications.

Primary Conclusion

The OPCD_OPW (5) formulation is considered optimal. It meets structural requirements (C25/30) while utilizing waste materials. Higher substitution levels result in unacceptable strength loss.

üéì PhD Relevance

Why this matters

Provides a clear "upper bound" (5%) for untreated organic aggregate replacement in structural concrete. High relevance for benchmarking agricultural waste valorization.

Recommended Actions

  • Replicate the 5% mix to verify the C25 threshold.
  • Extend parameter space to include chemical pre-treatment of OPA to potentially increase substitution limits.
  • Conduct durability tests (missing in this paper) to validate service life.

üå± Sustainability Impact

Waste Valorisation

Utilization of both solid (OPA) and liquid (OMW) waste from the olive industry, reducing landfill burden.

Resource Conservation

Reduces demand for natural sand (5% volume replacement), conserving river/quarry resources.
