Strict Summary of Findings

‚úì Extracted

This study characterized coal waste (CW) and olive pomace (OP) as potential raw materials for composite bricks. Materials were sieved into fractions (CW1, CW2, CW3, OP1, OP2) and analyzed via XRF, XRD, and FTIR. Results showed CW is rich in silica, alumina, and iron oxide, with quartz as the major phase. OP showed high calcium oxide (36.34%) and potassium oxide (19.08%) content, with minerals including gaylussite and portlandite. No composite bricks were fabricated or tested in this specific paper.
