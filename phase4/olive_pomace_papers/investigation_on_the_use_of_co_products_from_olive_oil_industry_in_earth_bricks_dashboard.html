üéì Core Research Contribution ‚ö° Interpretive

This review article evaluates the feasibility of valorizing Olive Mill Wastewater (OMWW) and Olive Pomace in the production of fired and unfired earth bricks. It highlights that the incorporation of these co-products generally improves thermal insulation properties (up to 20% reduction in conductivity) but may compromise mechanical strength and increase shrinkage. The paper synthesizes existing literature to propose these waste streams as viable additives for non-structural, eco-efficient building materials.

üî¨ Material Processing Context

The review notes that the form of the waste depends on the extraction process used in the industry:

  • Three-phase system: Produces separate Oil, Solid Pomace, and Liquid OMWW.
  • Two-phase system: Produces Oil and a semi-solid mixture (Wet Pomace/OPO).
Extractor Note: The paper highlights that these by-products are generally untreated before use in brick manufacturing studies.

‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Limitations

üìâ

High Shrinkage

Linear shrinkage increases drastically (up to +69%) with OMWW addition. This poses a significant risk of cracking during drying and firing.

üß±

Mechanical Strength Trade-off

While some conditions show improvement, high porosity generally tends to reduce mechanical resistance, limiting use to non-structural applications.
