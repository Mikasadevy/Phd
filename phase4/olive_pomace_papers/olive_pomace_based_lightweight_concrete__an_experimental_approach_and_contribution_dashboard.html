üåç Problem Definition

Conventional aggregate resources are depleting while the Algerian industry produces huge quantities of olive-pomace every year.

Executive Summary

The authors investigated the use of olive pomace as a lightweight aggregate in concrete. Olive pomace from Sig, Algeria, with a density of 0.9 g/cm¬≥ and mean particle diameter of 8mm, was pre-wetted (35% absorption) before mixing. Concrete cylinders (16x32cm) were prepared with varying gravel/sand (G/S) ratios from 0.6 to 1.2 and olive pomace substitution rates of 15%, 40%, and 60%. Results indicated that olive pomace concrete has lower compressive strength than conventional concrete due to poor adhesion between the aggregate and cement matrix. An optimum rate of 35% substitution was claimed.

Core Takeaway: Olive pomace aggregates (0.9 g/cm¬≥) were used to produce lightweight concrete, but poor matrix adhesion resulted in reduced compressive strength compared to conventional concrete.
