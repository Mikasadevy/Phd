üéØ Research Objective

To investigate the influence of incorporating carbon footprint-reducing additives (bio-based OP/Straw and industrial FA) on the hygrothermal and mechanical behavior of gypsum plaster-based material.

üö® Problem Statement

The olive oil industry generates ~20 million tonnes of solid olive pomace annually, causing environmental pollution. There is a lack of experimental data on the hygric characteristics of gypsum composites incorporating this waste.

üìä Executive Summary of Results

Incorporating 25% olive pomace reduces thermal conductivity by 38% (0.5 to 0.31 W/mK) and increases moisture buffering capacity fivefold (MBV 0.57 to 2.65), but reduces compressive strength by 80% (7.4 to 1.5 MPa).
