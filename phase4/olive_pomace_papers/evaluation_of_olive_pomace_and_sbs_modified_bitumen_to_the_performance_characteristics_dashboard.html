One-Sentence Takeaway

Addition of 19% olive pomace by weight to bitumen yields rheological and mechanical performance comparable to 4% SBS polymer modification with lower cost.

Strict Summary: The authors modified B 50/70 bitumen with 4-30% Olive Pomace (OP) and compared it to 4-4.5% SBS modified bitumen. OP addition decreased penetration and increased softening point. Rheological testing (DSR, BBR) and Marshall stability tests identified 19% OP as the critical ratio providing performance comparable to 4% SBS, specifically in rutting and fatigue resistance. Cost analysis indicated OP modified bitumen was more economical than SBS modification.
