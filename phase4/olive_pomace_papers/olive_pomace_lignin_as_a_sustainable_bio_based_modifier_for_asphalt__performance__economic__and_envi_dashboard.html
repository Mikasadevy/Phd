Research Objective

Investigates the potential of olive pomace lignin (OPL), extracted via acid hydrolysis, as a sustainable and eco-friendly modifier for asphalt binders and concrete mixtures. The study aims to replace fossil-based bitumen and improve mechanical performance.

Problem Definition

  • Environmental pollution from agricultural waste.
  • Depletion of fossil resources (bitumen).
  • Need for renewable road materials.

Solution Proposed

  • Extraction of lignin from olive pomace.
  • Modification of AC 60/70 binder with 4-12% OPL.
  • Validation via Marshall Stability and Resilient Modulus.

Technical Risks

  • Brittleness: Increased stiffness at high dosages (>10%) may lead to low-temperature cracking.
  • Ductility Loss: Significant reduction in ductility indicates reduced flexibility.

Data Limitations

  • Material Variability: Biomass source (olive mill type) can affect lignin purity.
  • Missing Long-term Data: PAV (Long-term aging) and low-temperature cracking (TSR) data absent.
